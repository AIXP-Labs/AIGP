# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge: Axiom 0

In the interest of fostering an open, safe, and rigorous environment for building generation infrastructure for autonomous AI, we pledge to prioritize **Human Sovereignty and Wellbeing** above all experimental, commercial, or computational goals.

This pledge is known as **Axiom 0** — the immovable axiom AINP aligns to (AINP does not own it; HSAW does — see [AINP_Protocol.md](specification/AINP_Protocol.md) §2).

## Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other contributors
- Enforcing technical rigor and honest acknowledgment of limits in reviews
- Gracefully accepting constructive feedback on protocol design
- Being transparent about what AINP can and cannot do (it is a generation-plan layer between intent (AIIP) and work (AIJP) — a blueprint/acceptance contract, not a generator, executor, runtime, or trust root)
- Keeping the English (`docs/`) and Chinese (`docs_cn/`) materials in parity

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Proposing modifications that compromise, dilute, or bypass **Axiom 0** (HSAW) — for example, removing or making optional the high-risk human approval gate (G6), the red-line assurance limits (G14/G15), the plan-as-untrusted-input security rules, or the disclosure requirements (G8) ([AINP_Protocol.md](specification/AINP_Protocol.md) §7 / §10)
- Designing generation plans or tooling that launder unverified self-claims into trust marks, strip disclosure/content credentials downstream, or bypass consent requirements for likeness/biometric/minor-related content ([AINP_Protocol.md](specification/AINP_Protocol.md) §7)
- Passing off failed or unverified work as successful/verified, hiding a plan revision, suppressing a feedback record, or otherwise defeating the governance, acceptance, or feedback model
- Inflating the protocol's capabilities or citing unverified technical claims (citations MUST be verifiable; see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md))
- Public or private harassment of other contributors
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including GitHub repositories, discussions, issues, pull requests, and any other communication channels associated with the AINP project.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

The AINP Protocol maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior.

Any protocol change request determined to weaken Axiom 0 — to compromise, dilute, or make optional the alignment to HSAW — will be rejected regardless of performance benefits, commercial pressure, or technical convenience.

## Reporting

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the AINP Protocol maintainers via the contact information in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.
